Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy & Cookies Notice (“Notice”) describes how Bragg Live Food Products, LLC (hereinafter “Bragg”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) collects, uses, and discloses information about you when you access or use any portion of our website and the information and services made available to you when you visit our website (the “Site”). The types of information we collect depend on how you use our Site. We may update this Notice at any time, and we will indicate at the top of this page the date it was last revised. If you have any questions about this Notice, please do not hesitate to contact us at privacy@bragg.com

Types of Information Collected by Bragg

The most common information we collect is the information you provide us. For instance, when you create an account with us or contact us through the online portal provided on our Site to ask questions or subscribe to our newsletter, we ask you to provide certain personal information, such as your name, company, email address, location, and other contact details. If you contact us by phone, email, social media, or by some other means, we may keep a record of that correspondence and any information that you submitted in connection therewith. When we send you emails, including newsletters, we may also track if you open them. We may also collect other content and information you share by commenting on or participating in online forums and similar social media communities, or when you otherwise interact with us by any other means. If you want to purchase our products from the Site, it is our policy to redirect you to a third-party merchant. The Site may ask you to enter information about your location in order to direct you to the retailer nearest to you. Or the Site may direct you to a third-party online retailer. Please refer to those third-parties’ privacy policies and terms of use to learn more about their privacy practices, which we do not control. You may also purchase our products over the phone with one of Bragg’s customer service representatives. The phone representative may request certain personal information in order to complete the transaction, such as your name, company, email address, shipping address, phone number and other contact details. They may also request payment information, such as your credit or debit card number or your bank account number in order to process payments from you or to remit payments to you in connection with your purchase. When you make a purchase or return, we collect information about the transaction, such as product details, purchase price, and date and location of the transaction.

We (and our service providers) may collect information about how you use and interact with the Site using various technologies, such as cookies or web beacons. A cookie is a small text file that any website, including ours, places in your internet browser to save your preferences. We may use cookies to collect information about your location, your browser, when you accessed the Site and for how long, the website address that linked you to our Site, and the website you visited after leaving our Site. We may also use cookies to share information with other websites, media partners and social networks in order to offer you pertinent promotional and adverting communications that match your interests (third party cookies). Most browsers allow you to block and delete cookies. You may disable the installation of cookies by configuring your browser appropriately. However, if you do that, please be aware that the Site may not work properly. If you do not wish Bragg to use all or part of the above-mentioned cookies, please contact us at privacy@bragg.com. We may also utilize web analytics to help us improve our Site and increase website traffic. For example, we may use Google Analytics or other similar service providers to track and analyze our website data such as how many visitors we are getting, how long they stayed on the Site, their browsing habits, and their geographical location. Examining data from web analytics helps us understand who typically visits our Site, how they get to our Site, and what parts of are Site are viewed or visited most often. Understanding our audience helps us improve the products and services we provide to our customers.

When and Why Bragg May Use Your Information

We use your information for a variety of business and commercial purposes, such as for the purpose of: processing or fulfilling orders and transactions; providing customer service; marketing and advertising; internal research; providing, maintaining and improving our products and services; understanding our audience and improving the Site; communicating with you about our products; analyzing trends and usage of our Site; investigating and preventing fraud or other illegal activities, including any violation of our Terms of Use; maintaining appropriate records for internal administrative and accounting purposes; and for any other purpose communicated to you at the time the information was collected or as permitted by law.

When and Why Bragg May Share your Information with Others

We may share your information with other companies, third-party merchants, vendors, consultants and other service providers to assist us with the overall operation of our company. Our business operations rely on many third-party vendors that offer a variety of helpful services such as: marketing and advertising, logistics; order fulfillment; shipping; web hosting; ecommerce and online selling platforms; internet infrastructures; cloud computing; payment collection and processing; site analytics and optimization; inbound and outbound email services; spam filtering; social networking; and both direct and computer-mediated services that facilitate the creation or sharing of information, ideas, interests and other forms of expression related to the promotion and sale of our products. Please refer to these third-parties’ privacy policies and terms of use to learn more about their privacy practices, which we do not control. We cannot control or be held responsible for third parties’ privacy practices. There is also a chance we may share your personal information with others in order to: comply with government regulations, respond to court orders, comply with subpoenas or warrants, or adhere to other legal requirements; facilitate internal audits, company valuation or related internal due diligence; assist with the negotiation of or actual change of control or ownership in the company due to investment, merger, sale of company assets, financing or acquisition; and to personalize advertisements or connect you with merchants we believe you may be interested in and vice versa.

Data security.

We protect your personal data with various technical and organizational security measures that aim to minimize risks associated with data loss and the unauthorized use, disclosure and alteration of data. To this end, we use technology like firewalls and data encryption. We also restrict access to data with authorization controls for data access. Additionally, you should take all steps necessary to maintain the privacy of your accounts and related log-in and password information as well as restrict access by others to your computer devices. Our privacy and security features have limits. If you suspect that someone other than you has accessed your account, email, or otherwise acted in a fraudulent way insofar as it concerns your relationship with Bragg, please notify us immediately.

Your Choices

Many web browsers accept cookies by default. You have the choice to remove or reject browser cookies. Please note that if you choose to remove or reject cookies, this could affect your use of and the functionality of our Site. Further, we only want to communicate with those who want to hear from us. You have the right to unsubscribe from our emails and newsletters at any time by simply clicking the designated “unsubscribe” link. If you would like to delete, update or correct your email address, mailing address, phone number or other contact information you provided us, please contact us at privacy@bragg.com. Requests to remove or update your contact information may take up to fifteen business days.

Storage of Data

We may store data until it is no longer necessary. This determination is made on a case-by-case basis and depends on the nature of the data. For example, it may be necessary to store personal data regarding pending or future legal disputes, or for things like maintaining and improving our Site and our products, or for keeping our systems secure, or for maintaining appropriate business and financial records. Personal data contained in contracts, documents, notifications and other business correspondence may also be subject to statutory storage obligations depending on the law in the applicable region.

Links to Third Party Websites

Our Site may contain links to websites operated and maintained by third parties, over which we have no control, including social media websites hosted by third-party providers. However, this Privacy Notice only addresses Bragg’s collection and use of your information, if any. Your access to such third-party websites is solely at your own risk. We have authorized distributors located outside of the United States. Your information may be transferred, transmitted to, stored, or processed in the United States or other areas located outside your country as necessary for us to provide our Products to consumers throughout the world.
